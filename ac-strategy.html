Association Croquet Opening Strategy

Master the opening moves of Association Croquet with this decision tree. Understanding openings and responses is fundamental to competitive AC play.

TURN 1 First Ball (Blue) Placement

EAST BOUNDARY (H4-H5)
Defensive, non-threatening. Lets opponent dictate game style. Best for stronger players seeking defensive positioning.
IN-LAWN EAST BOUNDARY
Standard east placement but 1 yard inward. Allows Black to play behind Blue on turn 3, discourages aggressive west boundary responses.
SUPERSHOT (Center court, 2 yds W of centerline)
Most aggressive sensible option. Signals "no grey area." Creates break opportunities for either side.
ANTI-DUFFER (H6-peg, 1-2 yds W of E boundary)
Discourages "duffer tice" responses. Gives Black strong turn 3 break potential if opponent attempts defensive play.

TURN 2 Red Responses to Blue

Responses to East Boundary Ball

STANDARD TICE
West boundary, 8-13 yards north of C1. Distance should match opponent's critical distance (50% hit rate) ±1 yard.
C2 BALL
6 inches south of C2. Defensive positioning preventing double targets if Black misses turn 3.
DUFFER TICE
Near penultimate (2 feet north, few inches east). Aggressive: encourages gentle miss toward A baulk.
C4 BALL
6 inches north of C4. Aggressive, New Zealand-style opening signaling fourth-turn break intent.
SHOOT BLUE
Direct attack. Risk: misses may leave triple targets for Yellow.

Responses to Supershot Ball

C2 RESPONSE
6 inches south of C2. Requires 13-yard Black shot; miss leaves double target for Yellow.
C4 RESPONSE
Mirror of C2, 6 inches north of C4. Use when peg-high or when H4 blocks croquet angles.
MAX DISTANCE
East boundary near peg level. Counter against elite players — longest possible shot for Black.
SUPERSHOT LAG
3-4 yards east, 1-2 yards north of supershot ball. Nullifies shooting strength.
ROQUET SUPERSHOT
Hit it! Ultimate nullification. Croquet options: Blue to C2, northwest of H2, or wiring at peg.

TURN 3 Black Responses

After Standard West Boundary Tice

SHOOT BLUE from A baulk → Miss leaves free shot for Yellow
JOIN WITH RED from C3 toward C4 → Avoid double targets
SHOOT RED directly → Hit achieves dream leave; miss toward C2 gives free return shot

After C2 Ball

SHOOT PARTNER (preferred) from A baulk leaving double target; or from C3 avoiding doubles
SHOOT RED only viable if exactly cornered
JOIN NEAR RED 3-4 feet south, no double target, good break prospects if Yellow misses

After C4 Ball

SHOOT PARTNER → Hit = dream leave; miss = 13-yard Yellow shot
SHOOT C4 FROM A BAULK → Hit = dream leave; miss = tricky double
JOIN NORTH OF C4 → No double, still 13-yard Yellow shot
BLACK TO C2 → Neutralize aggression; Yellow misses yield 9-yard response

After Duffer Tice

SHOOT RED (common) — Most aggressive, reasonable break if hit
SHOOT FROM C3 — Defensive, miss toward west boundary avoids Yellow break
SHOOT PARTNER FROM A BAULK — Big croquet stroke to H2, risky against weak play

TURN 4 Yellow Responses

IGNORED WEST BOUNDARY TICE
Shoot Red from C1 (~50% hit rate). Success: roll Red toward H2 and Blue/Black.
MISSED WEST BOUNDARY TICE
Shoot Red from east of C1, joining if missed. Hit: roll toward C1 for next-turn rush.
IGNORED C2 BALL
Aggressive: shoot Blue/Black double from A or B baulk. Defensive: shoot C3 to C4. Weaker player: shoot 13-yard Red.
IGNORED DUFFER TICE
Default: shoot Red from B baulk (9-10 yard shortest shot). Alternative: shoot Blue/Black double from A baulk if joined.

Strategic Framework

Match-up Recommended Style Key Tactics
Stronger vs Weaker Defensive Grind, avoid grey areas, force opponent errors
Weaker vs Stronger Aggressive Accept short-shot risks, favor in-lawn balls, pressure opponent
Evenly Matched Personal preference Elite players trend toward aggressive "black and white" openings

Target State: "Dream Leave"

  • Red: Slightly in-lawn from west boundary, north of peg-high
  • Blue: Positioned for rush toward Red
  • Yellow: No short shots available
  • Outcome: Yellow misses create Blue break opportunities

Quick Reference: Opening Signals

Opening Signal Risk Level
East Boundary Defensive, let opponent lead Low
Supershot "No grey area" — decisive game High
C2 Response Defensive counter Low
C4 Response Aggressive, NZ-style High
Duffer Tice Aggressive trap Medium-High
Standard Tice Neutral, test opponent Medium
