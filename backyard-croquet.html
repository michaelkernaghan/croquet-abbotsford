9-Wicket Backyard Croquet

Nine-wicket croquet is the classic American backyard game, perfect for family gatherings, picnics, and casual outdoor fun. This guide covers everything you need to know to set up and play.

Equipment Needed

  • 9 wickets (wire hoops)
  • 2 stakes (one at each end)
  • 6 balls in different colors
  • 6 mallets (one per player)

Ball Colors & Play Order

Blue (1st)
Red (2nd)
Black (3rd)
Yellow (4th)
Green (5th)
Orange (6th)

The colored bands on the stakes show the play order from top to bottom.

Court Setup

A standard backyard court is approximately 50 feet long by 25 feet wide, but you can adjust to fit your space. The court should be set up in a double-diamond pattern:

Court diagram courtesy of Backyard Sidekick

Object of the Game

Be the first player (or team) to navigate your ball through all 9 wickets in the correct order, hit the far stake, then return through all 9 wickets in reverse order, and finally hit the starting stake. This is a total of 14 points (9 wickets out + turning stake + 9 wickets back + finishing stake = but the stakes don't count as separate points in some variations).

The Wicket Sequence

Starting from the first stake, navigate through the wickets in this order:

Outward Journey:

  1. First wicket (in front of starting stake)
  2. Left wicket of first side group
  3. Center wicket of first side group
  4. Right wicket of first side group
  5. Center court wicket
  6. Left wicket of second side group
  7. Center wicket of second side group
  8. Right wicket of second side group
  9. Last wicket (before turning stake)

Then: Hit the turning stake and return through wickets 9 through 1 in reverse order, finishing by hitting the starting stake.

Basic Rules of Play

Starting the Game

  • All balls start from a mallet's length in front of the first stake
  • Players take turns in the color order shown on the stake
  • Each turn consists of one stroke, unless bonus strokes are earned

Earning Bonus Strokes

  • Running a wicket: When your ball passes completely through its correct wicket in the proper direction, you earn 1 bonus stroke
  • Hitting another ball (roquet): When your ball hits another ball, you earn 2 bonus strokes

Taking a Roquet (Hitting Another Ball)

When you hit another ball, you have two options for your bonus strokes:

  1. Take croquet: Place your ball touching the ball you hit, then strike your ball (this is the "croquet stroke"). You get one more stroke after that.
  2. Play from where your ball stopped: Simply take your two bonus strokes from your ball's current position.

Note: You can only roquet each ball once per turn. After running your next wicket, you can roquet them again.

The Croquet Stroke

When taking croquet, place your ball next to the ball you hit (touching it), then strike your ball. There are several techniques:

  • Drive shot: Both balls travel in the same direction
  • Split shot: Balls travel in different directions
  • Stop shot: Your ball stays relatively still while sending the other ball away

Foot shot rule: In casual backyard play, you may place your foot on your ball while taking croquet to keep it stationary. This makes sending the opponent's ball easier!

Deadness (Optional Advanced Rule)

In traditional 9-wicket croquet, once you roquet a ball, you are "dead" on that ball until you run your next wicket. This means you cannot earn bonus strokes by hitting that same ball again until you've scored.

For casual play, many families skip this rule and allow unlimited roquets.

Boundaries

  • If a ball goes out of bounds, it is placed one mallet's length inside the boundary where it went out
  • In casual play, you may simply place it where it exited

Winning the Game

The first player to complete the full course (out through all wickets, hit the turning stake, back through all wickets, and hit the starting stake) wins!

Team Play

For teams of 2 or 3 players:

  • 2 players per team: Partners play balls that are next to each other in order (Blue/Red vs Black/Yellow, or Blue/Black vs Red/Yellow)
  • 3 players per team: One team plays Blue/Black/Green, the other plays Red/Yellow/Orange
  • The team whose players all finish first wins

Poison Ball (Optional Variation)

A popular backyard variation! When a player completes the course but doesn't hit the final stake:

  • They become a "poison ball"
  • If a poison ball hits another ball, that ball is eliminated from the game
  • If a poison ball runs any wicket (even accidentally), it is eliminated
  • Last ball standing wins!

This variation adds exciting end-game drama and is great for parties!

Quick Reference Summary

Action Bonus Strokes
Run a wicket +1 stroke
Hit another ball (roquet) +2 strokes
Hit the turning stake +1 stroke
Run wicket + hit ball in same stroke +3 strokes total
