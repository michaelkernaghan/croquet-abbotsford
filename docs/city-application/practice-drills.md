# Croquet Practice Drills
## For Introduction to Croquet Course - Adults & Seniors

---

## Beginner Drills (Weeks 1-2)

### Drill 1: Stance and Grip Check
**Purpose:** Establish proper fundamentals
**Time:** 5 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 mallet per person

1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, ball centered between feet
2. Grip mallet with hands together, thumbs pointing down the shaft
3. Practice pendulum swing without hitting a ball
4. Focus on smooth backswing and follow-through
5. Instructor checks each participant's stance and grip

**Key Points:**
- Relaxed grip, not too tight
- Eyes on the ball
- Swing from shoulders, not wrists

---

### Drill 2: Straight Shot - 1 Metre
**Purpose:** Build confidence with short, straight shots
**Time:** 10 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 mallet, 2 balls per person

1. Place ball 1 metre from a hoop
2. Aim to hit ball through centre of hoop
3. Retrieve ball and repeat 10 times
4. Count successful shots through the hoop
5. Move to 1.5 metres when achieving 7/10 success rate

**Progression:**
- 1 metre → 1.5 metres → 2 metres

---

### Drill 3: Stop and Stare
**Purpose:** Develop follow-through and head stillness
**Time:** 5 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 mallet, 1 ball per person

1. Set up a straight 2-metre shot to a hoop
2. Hit the ball and freeze position
3. Count to three before looking up to see result
4. Repeat 10 times

**Why It Works:**
Lifting your head too early pulls the shot off line. This drill trains patience and proper form.

## Intermediate Drills (Weeks 3-4)

### Drill 4: The Clock Face
**Purpose:** Practice hoop approaches from multiple angles
**Time:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 mallet, 1 ball per person, 1 hoop

1. Place markers at 12, 3, 6, and 9 o'clock positions around a hoop (2 metres out)
2. Shoot from 12 o'clock through the hoop
3. Move to 3 o'clock, then 6, then 9
4. Complete 3 full rotations
5. Track success rate at each position

**Scoring:**
- Note which positions are strongest/weakest
- Focus extra practice on weaker angles

---

### Drill 5: The Ladder
**Purpose:** Build distance control
**Time:** 10 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 mallet, 1 ball per person, 5 markers

1. Set up markers at 1m, 2m, 3m, 4m, and 5m from start position
2. Hit ball to stop as close to 1m marker as possible
3. Progress through each distance
4. Return back down: 5m, 4m, 3m, 2m, 1m
5. Score: 2 points within 15cm, 1 point within 30cm

**Goal:** Complete the ladder with 15+ points

---

### Drill 6: Roquet Practice
**Purpose:** Learn to hit a target ball
**Time:** 10 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 mallet, 2 balls per person

1. Place target ball (red) 2 metres away
2. Hit striker ball (blue) to contact the red ball
3. Successful roquet = hit the target
4. After each hit, reset and try again
5. Complete 10 attempts, track success rate

**Progression:**
- 2 metres → 3 metres → 4 metres
- Move target ball to different angles

---

### Drill 7: Two-Ball Sequence
**Purpose:** Practice continuation shots after roquet
**Time:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 mallet, 2 balls per person

1. Place target ball 2 metres from a hoop
2. Place striker ball 2 metres behind target ball
3. Roquet the target ball
4. Take croquet shot to position both balls
5. Run the hoop with continuation shot

**Focus:** Understanding the two-shot sequence after a roquet

---

## Advanced Drills (Weeks 5-6)

### Drill 8: The Triangle
**Purpose:** Practice three-ball positioning
**Time:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 mallet, 3 balls

1. Set up balls in triangle formation near a hoop
2. Roquet first ball, take croquet
3. Roquet second ball, take croquet
4. Position to run hoop
5. Run the hoop

**Goal:** Complete the sequence in minimum strokes

---

### Drill 9: Hoop and Position
**Purpose:** Score hoop while setting up next shot
**Time:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 mallet, 2 balls, 2 hoops

1. Set up two hoops in sequence (as on court)
2. Run first hoop
3. After running hoop, ball should be positioned toward second hoop
4. Approach and run second hoop
5. Track how many strokes to complete both hoops

**Target:** Complete both hoops in 4 strokes or fewer

---

### Drill 10: Croquet Shot Control
**Purpose:** Master the croquet shot (hitting two balls)
**Time:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 mallet, 2 balls, markers

1. Place balls touching (as after a roquet)
2. Place foot on striker ball (optional, for beginners)
3. Hit striker ball to send both balls in desired directions
4. Practice sending partner ball:
   - Straight ahead (drive shot)
   - At 45 degrees (split shot)
   - Barely moving (stop shot)

**Key Learning:** Angle of mallet face and force control results

---

### Drill 11: Tactical Positioning
**Purpose:** Think strategically about ball placement
**Time:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** Full court setup, 4 balls

1. Set up a game scenario mid-court
2. Instructor describes tactical goal (e.g., "Get to hoop 3 while blocking opponent")
3. Participant plans and executes 3 shots
4. Group discusses alternatives and outcomes
5. Rotate through different scenarios

**Scenarios:**
- Approach a hoop with good leave
- Block opponent's path to their hoop
- Set up a break (sequence of hoops)

## Warm-Up Routines

### 5-Minute Quick Warm-Up
1. 10 practice swings (no ball)
2. 5 straight shots at 1 metre
3. 5 shots at 2 metres
4. Ready to play

### 10-Minute Full Warm-Up
1. 10 practice swings focusing on rhythm
2. Straight shots: 5 at 1m, 5 at 2m, 5 at 3m
3. Clock face drill: 1 shot from each position
4. 3 roquet attempts at 3 metres
5. Ready to play

---

## Practice Games

### Game 1: First to Five
**Players:** 2-4
**Time:** 15-20 minutes

- Each player takes turns shooting at the same hoop
- First player to score 5 hoops wins
- After scoring, return to 2-metre starting position
- Good for: Building hoop-running confidence

### Game 2: Roquet Rally
**Players:** 2
**Time:** 10 minutes

- Players take turns trying to roquet opponent's ball
- Successful roquet = 1 point
- After roquet, both balls reset to 3 metres apart
- First to 10 points wins
- Good for: Improving accuracy

### Game 3: Golf Croquet (Simplified)
**Players:** 2-4
**Time:** 20-30 minutes

- Play first 6 hoops only
- Each hoop is contested (first to score wins the hoop)
- No croquet shots, just alternate turns
- First to 4 hoops wins
- Good for: Learning game flow and tactics

## Tips for Instructors

**For Seniors:**
- Allow seated rest between drills
- Provide lighter mallets if available
- Keep drill distances shorter initially
- Focus on enjoyment over competition
- Allow modifications for mobility limitations

**For All Participants:**
- Demonstrate each drill before starting
- Circulate and provide individual feedback
- Celebrate improvements, not just successes
- Keep energy positive and encouraging
- End each session with a fun game element

**Equipment Check:**
- Mallets appropriate height for each participant
- Balls clean and visible
- Hoops firmly set in ground
- Boundary markers visible
- First aid kit accessible
